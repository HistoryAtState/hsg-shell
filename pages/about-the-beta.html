About the Beta

The Office of the Historian is working on a new version of history.state.gov. In order to gather feedback before the website is officially released, we are sharing the new site in “beta”—or preview form—at history.state.gov/beta. Please have a look around and send us your suggestions via email at history@state.gov or on Twitter at @HistoryAtState.

Please forgive our warts during this period. Certain features are incomplete and known to contain bugs. The data here is not updated as regularly as the main site. So please visit the original version of the site for its content.

Features Currently on Display

  • A mobile device-friendly “responsive” design: Adapts to the size of your device and is readable on any screen size, from smart phone to tablet and desktop. (On a computer, try making your browser window smaller, or, on a mobile device, try changing your screen’s orientation; notice how the elements of the page adapt to the new size.) The new design is aligned with U.S. Web Design Standards.
  • More fluid reading experience: Quickly advance forward or backward in publications such as the Foreign Relations series. To try this out, visit this document and select the “previous” or “next” arrows to the left and right of the document.
  • Security: Your connection to the beta site—and all of history.state.gov—is now encrypted. This setting, called HSTS, forces all connections to the site to use a secure, HTTPS connection, ensuring the integrity of the pages you visit and preventing others from tracking which pages you visit or what terms you search for. For more on this standard and its adoption by U.S. Government websites, see https.cio.gov.
  • A more stable, better performing server, with all code and data available for all to download, developed in the open: We have rewritten the entire site from scratch with the goal of delivering results faster and sustaining increasing loads much better. All code and datasets on this site can be accessed from our HistoryAtState GitHub repository and that of the core web application, hsg-shell and deployment. At that link you can follow our progress, fork our code, file issues, send us your pull requests. The hsg-project repository contains all instructions to install a complete copy of the site on your own computer.

Known Issues

Before the new site replaces the old one, we will be addressing the following known issues. (A fuller list of issues is on the GitHub project site, hsg-shell.) Until then, please use the button at the top of this page to reach the current site for these features.

  • The site-wide search is limited to the Foreign Relations series.
  • Links to ebooks and PDFs of Foreign Relations volumes from each volume’s landing page are not yet available. Ebooks can be accessed from the main ebooks page.
  • The OPDS ebook catalog API and atom feeds are not yet implemented.
  • The photo gallery from the Southeast Asia conference is not yet implemented.

Features Planned for Future Releases

Besides these features, we are laying the foundation for new resources on the history of U.S. foreign relations and the Department of State:

  • A unified biographical database of all persons identified in datasets such as the Foreign Relations series, Principal Officers and Chiefs of Mission, Travels of the President and Secretary of State, and Visits of Foreign Leaders.
  • A database of historical country names, for connecting resources about places whose names have changed over time.
  • Chronological sorting of search results from the Foreign Relations series.

Thank you for your interest in this beta, and please return for updates.
